The [Intraclass Correlation Coefficient (ICC)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intraclass_correlation) is a metric indicating the proportion of variance attributable to a grouping variable in a dataset. The formula for the ICC is:
$$
\text{ICC} = \frac{\sigma_0^2}{\sigma_{total}^2}
$$
where $\sigma_{0}^2$ represents the between-group variance.
